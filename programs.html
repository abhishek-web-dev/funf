FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick guide answers to standard Germany study queries.

Public universities in Germany are state-funded and do not charge tuition fees. Students only pay a small semester administrative contribution (Semesterbeitrag) ranging from €150 to €350 per semester, which often includes a local transport ticket.

Yes. The German Academic Exchange Service (DAAD) is the primary scholarship provider offering merit-based financial aid. Additionally, individual universities, political foundations, and the Deutschlandstipendium offer monthly financial aids.

For a Master's degree, you need a relevant Bachelor's degree with good ECTS credits. For a Bachelor's degree, candidates usually require Class 12 board marks plus either a year of college in India, passing JEE Advanced, or completing a foundation Studienkolleg course in Germany.

To secure a German study visa, you must show financial proof. This is done by opening a blocked bank account (Sperrkonto) and depositing the official yearly living expenses (approx. €11,900 for 2026). Once this is locked, you book a visa appointment at the VFS/Embassy with your university admission letter.

Ausbildung is a dual vocational training format combining classroom theory with practical corporate work. In fields like nursing or IT, you receive a monthly stipend ranging from €1,000 to €1,400 depending on the sector, which covers living costs while you study.

For English-taught Masters, you need IELTS (usually 6.5) and basic German (A1/A2 is highly recommended for daily life). For German-taught degrees, a minimum of B2 or C1 TestDaF/Goethe is mandatory. For Ausbildung programs, B1 or B2 is strictly required for visa approval.
